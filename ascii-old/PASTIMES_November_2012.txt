PANHANDLE ARCHAEOLOGICAL SOCIETY
Minutes of the October 17, 2012 Meeting
-President Donna Otto called the meeting to order at 7:03 p.m. at the Wildcat Bluff Nature Center. A total of
18 members were in attendance; no guests were present.
-The next PAS meeting will be 7:00 p.m. on November 28, 2012 at the Wildcat Bluff Nature Center. Note:
This meeting will be on the fourth Wednesday of the month since the third Wednesday is the day before
Thanksgiving Day.
The meeting activity consisted of repairing spears to be used at the upcoming Fannin History Fair.
Business Meeting
-The business meeting was held at the beginning of the meeting followed by the above mentioned activity.
-Lisa Jackson submitted the Treasurer’s report. The current operating balance is $2,185.39. The previous
monthly expenditures were $46.33. Note: Operating balance in last month’s minutes was $2,234.22; difference is $48.83.
-Rolla Shaller submitted the Publication Committee report. A total of $335.25 in interest was moved from the
CD account to the general budget. The current balance of the general account is $1,180.26. The CD account
is currently $5,000.00.
Old Business
-President Donna Otto will continue to work on the PAS bylaws and will present her results at the next meeting.
-Flint Fest was held on September 29th and 30th. Approximately 200 people attended and participated in a variety of “hands on” activities.
-The Hutchinson County Museum and the Panhandle-Plains Historical Museum co-sponsored a series of lectures at the Borger City Hall on September 29th. Dr. Paul Carlson spoke about his new book entitled “Myth,
Memory, and Massacre: The Pease River Capture of Cynthia Ann Parker”. Dr. Holle Humphries spoke about
the Quanah Parker Trail Project. PAS member Alvin Lynn spoke about the first battle of Adobe Walls. PAS
member Lisa Jackson spoke about the second battle of Adobe Walls. Approximately 100 people attended the
event. The planned field trip to the first and second battle sites were canceled due to rain.
-The commemoration of the Battle of Palo Duro Canyon at Palo Duro Canyon on September 29 th was rained
out. PAS members Chris Larson and Frank Willmon represented the Frontier regiment at a booth at the visitor’s center despite the rain! Approximately 250 people visited the booth.

New Business
-Upcoming Archaeology Month events include field trips to the Alibates Ruins and Quarries each weekend in
October, the Fannin History Fair on October 20th, and the Perryton Family Archaeology Day on November 3rd.
-The Studer Banquet will be Saturday December 15th from 6:00 to 10:00 p.m. in the Hazelwood Room at the
Panhandle-Plains Historical Museum. The Stockyards will cater a roast beef dinner and dessert. Silent auction items are needed! It was suggested that Dr. Doug Scott be asked to be the guest speaker. Lisa Jackson
volunteered to produce the banquet program.
-The PAS field trip to Box Canyon is tentatively scheduled for Saturday November 10th. Scott will send out an
email to members to determine a final date. On whatever date is selected we are planning on meeting that
morning at Wildcat Bluff Nature Center at 9:00 a.m. Participants need to bring water and lunch. Alvin Lynn
is coordinating this event.
-Lisa Jackson asked for ideas for speakers for upcoming PAS meetings. Scott suggested contacting Paul Carlson to speak about his new book.
-The business meeting adjourned at 7:28 p.m.

Family Archaeology Day at Museum of the Plains - Perryton on Saturday, November 3, from 1:00 pm to 5:00 pm.
The goal of this event is to increase knowledge and appreciation of prehistoric and historic cultures that occupied the region prior to settlement
through “hands on” activities and games. The field day is open to people of all ages and will include educational stations that examine indigenous ,
artwork, games, contests, and traditional tool and technology demonstrations. Visitors and participants are encouraged to bring in personal prehistoric and historic artifact collections for identification by experts. Sponsored by Courson Archaeological Research and the Museum of the
Plains. Admission is free, but donations are accepted.
Call Scott Brosowske at 806-434-0157 or the museum at 806-435-6400 for more information.
